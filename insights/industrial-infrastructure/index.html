Industrial Development & Infrastructure

Proximity to logistics networks is the backbone of successful ground-up industrial projects.

Industrial real estate rises and falls with infrastructure. Access to highways, ports, intermodals, and even last-mile distribution hubs can make or break a development.

Key Factors

  • Highways: Direct access to regional and interstate routes reduces transport costs.
  • Ports & Rail: Import/export and bulk distribution depend on intermodal efficiency.
  • Labor & Utilities: Workforce density and reliable utilities ensure operations scale.

Understanding these dynamics allows investors to fill gaps in shallow-bay or tilt-wall supply, and to deliver buildings sized and priced to actual market demand.
