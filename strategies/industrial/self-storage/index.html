Self Storage

Capturing resilient demand with climate-controlled and drive-up facilities in Texas growth corridors.

Our Approach

We pursue self storage in markets where rooftops outpace supply and visibility drives tenant acquisition. Facilities balance climate-controlled units, drive-up access, and strong branding to generate recurring income and high tenant retention. This strategy delivers durable returns with low operating costs and defensive qualities through cycles.

Where We Focus

Population growth corridors in the Texas Triangle — Houston, Dallas–Fort Worth, Austin, and San Antonio — along with suburban and infill locations near retail corridors, highways, and rooftops that drive consistent demand.

Execution Levers

  • Ground-up development of climate-controlled and drive-up facilities
  • Value-add repositioning: upgrading older sites with technology, security, and branding
  • Lease-up strategies using digital marketing, dynamic pricing, and cross-selling
  • Third-party partnerships with major operators for scalable management

Proven Playbook

Self storage has institutionalized as a core real estate class, offering sticky demand driven by life events and small businesses. Ownership fragmentation creates opportunities to consolidate and professionalize assets. We apply the same clarity and discipline from Deal Studio to this sector, ensuring underwriting precision and repeatability.

Self Storage in Practice

Self Storage — FAQs

What makes self storage attractive in Texas?

High population growth, limited supply, and resilient tenant demand make Texas one of the strongest markets for self storage.

Do you develop or acquire?

We pursue both ground-up development in undersupplied markets and value-add acquisitions where upgrades unlock rental growth.

How do you compete with REITs?

We target submarkets and deal sizes below the REIT threshold, leveraging disciplined execution and flexible capital structures.

Where do you focus new developments?

Primarily in growth corridors across the Texas Triangle, with suburban and infill sites near rooftops, retail, and highway access.
